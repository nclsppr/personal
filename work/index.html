Work

Platforms, decisions and leadership.

A closer look at the platforms I have helped build, the engineering decisions behind them, and the way I lead teams responsible for critical systems.

Industrial Connected Home platform#

1M+ connected devices · Worldline

Context

A platform used by major European home-appliance manufacturers, including Gorenje and Bosch/Siemens, connected appliances, cloud services and mobile applications.

Challenge

The platform had to manage more than one million devices simultaneously while supporting real-time communication, high availability and customers across several European countries.

My role

I contributed to the platform’s design and evolution, rebuilt backend services, led its cloud migration and coordinated a Romanian nearshore team and customers in Slovenia, Austria, Germany and Spain.

Key decisions

  • Use MQTT and mobile push notifications for a large-scale real-time notification system
  • Rebuild the backend around Spring Cloud microservices
  • Migrate toward Azure PaaS, Kubernetes and Docker
  • Keep architecture and production decisions understandable across distributed teams

Impact

The work improved platform scalability, observability and maintainability, and built my foundation in distributed systems, international collaboration and production engineering.

High-volume banking document factory#

10M reports / month · Worldline

Context

A document-generation platform produced PDF, AFP and PostScript communications from databases, XML files and reusable templates in a PCI-DSS-compliant environment.

Challenge

The platform needed reliable high-volume processing, maintainable document rendering and operational control for banking communications.

My role

As Technical Leader, I designed and built the platform, covering architecture, technology benchmarking, CI/CD, monitoring and production reliability.

Key decisions

  • Design a Java and microservices architecture
  • Benchmark rendering technologies for performance, quality and maintainability
  • Build an Angular dashboard for monitoring and operational control
  • Create an OpenAPI integration model reused by other Worldline teams

Impact

The platform reached 10 million banking reports processed per month. Continuous integration and deployment pipelines supported its delivery and operation.

Secure document & communication platforms#

Regulated environments · Victor Buck Services

Context

A portfolio of products creates, manages, secures and distributes confidential documents for financial institutions and governmental organizations.

Challenge

Identity services, customer portals, document production and multichannel distribution must evolve while supporting security, compliance and real business operations.

My role

I lead engineering work across the portfolio, coordinating development, infrastructure, architecture, security and business stakeholders while defining ownership and technical direction.

Key decisions

  • Evolve authentication and authorization services, including TOTP-based strong authentication
  • Modernize Java applications and support migrations toward modern frameworks and architectures
  • Define ownership across multiple products
  • Contribute to new central platforms for secure services and document management

Impact

The work strengthens shared ownership, technical direction and the evolution of secure identity, document and communication services.

Leadership in practice#

I lead a team of ten full-stack developers responsible for several business-critical products and shared technical platforms.

Clarifying ownership

I introduced clearer ownership and backup responsibilities. Identified engineers understand each major product’s direction, operational responsibilities and priorities, improving knowledge sharing, continuity and resilience.

Structuring portfolio priorities

I balance new development, legacy modernization, technical debt, security, production support and infrastructure dependencies. Close collaboration with product, architecture, security, operations and management makes constraints understandable on both sides.

Growing engineering autonomy

I encourage engineers to own products, decisions and stakeholder communication. I provide context and challenge assumptions instead of centralizing every decision, including when evaluating AI-assisted development within security, confidentiality and governance requirements.

Engineering principles#

01

Give people context, not just tasks

Engineers make better decisions when they understand the business, operational and technical context behind priorities.

02

Make ownership explicit

Clear ownership improves decision-making and prevents critical knowledge from remaining hidden.

03

Stay close to engineering reality

I stay involved in architecture, production issues and technical discussions, simplifying accumulated complexity where it helps.

04

Build teams, not heroes

Knowledge sharing, collective ownership and deliberate room for modernization reduce dependence on undocumented procedures and individual contributors.
