Work

Inside the platforms.

This page covers three systems I know first-hand: an industrial IoT platform, a banking document factory and a portfolio of secure services. It focuses on the decisions, constraints and team responsibilities I can discuss publicly.

Industrial Connected Home platform#

1M+ connected devices · Worldline

The system

A platform used by major European home-appliance manufacturers, including Gorenje and Bosch/Siemens, connected appliances, cloud services and mobile applications.

The problem

It had to manage more than one million devices simultaneously, handle real-time communication and remain available to customers across several European countries.

My contribution

I contributed to the platform’s design and evolution, rebuilt backend services, led its cloud migration and coordinated a Romanian nearshore team and customers in Slovenia, Austria, Germany and Spain.

Decisions

  • Designed the notification system around MQTT and mobile push notifications
  • Rebuilt the backend with Spring Cloud microservices
  • Led the migration to Azure PaaS, Kubernetes and Docker

Results

The platform managed more than one million connected devices. The work also improved its scalability, observability and maintainability.

High-volume banking document factory#

10M reports / month · Worldline

The system

A document-generation platform produced PDF, AFP and PostScript communications from databases, XML files and reusable templates in a PCI-DSS-compliant environment.

The problem

Banking communications required high-volume processing, consistent document rendering and enough operational control to run the platform in production.

My contribution

As Technical Leader, I designed and built the platform, covering architecture, technology benchmarking, CI/CD, monitoring and production reliability.

Decisions

  • Designed the Java and microservices architecture
  • Benchmarked rendering technologies for performance, quality and maintainability
  • Built an Angular dashboard for monitoring and operational control
  • Created an OpenAPI integration model reused by other Worldline teams

Results

The platform reached 10 million banking reports processed per month. Continuous integration and deployment pipelines supported its delivery and operation.

Secure document & communication platforms#

Regulated environments · Victor Buck Services

The portfolio

The products create, manage, secure and distribute confidential documents for financial institutions and governmental organizations.

The problem

Identity services, customer portals, document production and multichannel distribution must evolve without disrupting security, compliance or daily operations.

My contribution

I lead engineering work across the portfolio, coordinate development, infrastructure, architecture, security and business stakeholders, and make product ownership and technical direction explicit.

Current work

  • Evolving authentication and authorization services, including TOTP-based strong authentication
  • Modernizing Java applications and supporting migrations to modern frameworks and architectures
  • Defining ownership across multiple products
  • Contributing to central platforms for secure services and document management

Public outcomes

The concrete outcomes I can describe publicly are TOTP-based strong authentication, modernized Java applications, clearer product ownership and work on new central platforms.

Leadership in practice#

I lead a team of ten full-stack developers responsible for several business-critical products and shared technical platforms.

Clarifying ownership

I introduced clearer ownership and backup responsibilities. Identified engineers understand each major product’s direction, operational responsibilities and priorities, improving knowledge sharing, continuity and resilience.

Structuring portfolio priorities

I maintain priorities across new development, legacy modernization, technical debt, security, production support and infrastructure dependencies. I work with product owners, infrastructure, architects, security, operations and management to expose dependencies and trade-offs.

Growing engineering autonomy

Engineers own products, technical decisions and stakeholder communication. I provide context and question assumptions instead of centralizing every decision. The same approach guides our evaluation of AI-assisted development within security, confidentiality and governance requirements.

Engineering principles#

01

Share the context

Engineers make better decisions when they understand the business, operational and technical reasons behind a priority.

02

Name the owner

An explicit owner makes decisions easier and prevents critical knowledge from remaining hidden.

03

Stay close to production

I stay involved in architecture, production issues and technical discussions, and simplify accumulated complexity when it gets in the team’s way.

04

Share knowledge by default

Documentation, backups and collective ownership keep products from depending on one person or one undocumented command.
